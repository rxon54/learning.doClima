## Why it matters for doClima

These ARE the product. Knowing their internals means you can explain limitations, catch errors, and design better compound indicators.

## SEEG

- Published by Observatório do Clima (civil society)
- 5 sectors: Energy, Industry, Agriculture, [[lulucf]], Waste
- **Municipal data is ESTIMATED** for most sectors (proxy: population, GDP). Only LULUCF has direct municipal data.
- Top emitters are NOT big cities — they're Amazonian deforestation frontiers
- Your DB: `seeg_emissions.db` (~91 MB)

## MapBiomas

- Collection 9: 1985-2023, 30m resolution, Landsat-derived
- ~30 land use classes. Forest classification = very reliable. Secondary vegetation = less so.
- Gross vs net deforestation distinction matters
- Your DB: `mapbiomas.db` (~294 MB)

## AdaptaBrasil

- MCTI, all 5,570 municipalities, normalized 0-1
- Two scenarios: optimistic (SSP2-4.5) and pessimistic (SSP5-8.5)
- Time horizons: 2030, 2040, 2050
- The `pessimist` flag handles counter-intuitive indicator directions

## Practical Exercise

Pick a Contradição Ampliada municipality (e.g. São Félix do Xingu/PA). Trace emissions by SEEG sector. Check MapBiomas dominant classes. Read AdaptaBrasil risk areas. Write a one-paragraph narrative connecting all three.
