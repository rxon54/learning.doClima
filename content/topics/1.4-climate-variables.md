## Why it matters for doClima

You derive indicators from raw climate data. Knowing what SPI is (vs "below average rainfall") and why [[wbgt]] matters more than air temperature makes your indicators defensible.

## Core Concepts

**Precipitation:**
- Accumulated mm, climatological normal (1991-2020 baseline), anomaly
- [[spi]] — Standardized Precipitation Index. Negative = dry. Below -2.0 = extreme drought.
- [[return-period]] — "1-in-100-year event" = 1% annual probability, not a 100-year cycle

**Temperature:**
- Tmax / Tmin — fundamental units
- Extreme heat day: Tmax above 90th/95th percentile
- Hot night: Tmin above threshold (bodies can't recover from daytime heat)
- [[wbgt]] — gold standard for heat stress. Above 35°C wet-bulb = lethal regardless of hydration.

**Drought types (sequential):**
- Meteorological (SPI) → Agricultural (soil moisture) → Hydrological (reservoir deficit)

**Extremes:**
- [[idf-curves]] — Intensity-Duration-Frequency: how much rain, how fast, how rare
- Compound events: heat + drought, rain + wind
