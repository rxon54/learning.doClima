# WBGT — Wet Bulb Globe Temperature

Gold standard for assessing heat stress on the human body. Combines air temperature, humidity, radiant heat, and wind speed into a single thermal stress index. Unlike "feels like" indices, WBGT has a hard physiological limit: above 35°C wet-bulb temperature, the human body cannot cool itself regardless of hydration or shade — this is lethal within hours.

**Thresholds for outdoor work:**
- 28°C WBGT: increased risk, limit heavy labor
- 32°C WBGT: dangerous for manual workers
- 35°C WBGT: lethal for all humans (Sherwood & Huber 2010)

**Why it matters for doClima:** Brazil's Semiárido and Amazon regions are approaching WBGT thresholds under SSP5-8.5. This is not a future risk — it's a near-present risk for agricultural and construction workers.
