# Reanalysis — Blended Historical Climate Records

A reanalysis reconstructs the state of the atmosphere over past decades by running a modern weather model and assimilating all available historical observations (weather stations, radiosondes, satellites). The result is a spatially complete, physically consistent gridded dataset.

**Not pure observations** — the model fills gaps. Biases exist especially for precipitation in complex terrain and for early decades (pre-1979) with fewer satellite observations.

**[[era5]]** is currently the gold standard global reanalysis.
