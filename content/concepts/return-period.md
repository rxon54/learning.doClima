# Return Period — How Rare is an Extreme Event?

The average time between occurrences of an event of a given magnitude. A "100-year flood" has a 1% annual exceedance probability — NOT a guarantee of happening exactly once per century. In any 100-year window, there's a 63% chance of at least one such event.

**Critical issue for doClima:** as climate changes, the magnitude associated with a given return period changes. The "100-year flood" of 1980 may now be a "20-year flood." Infrastructure designed for historical IDF curves is systematically underprepared.
